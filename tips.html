The Author field should contain your name. No matter who you are or who you are putting the ticket in for, you are the author and your name is required.
The Barcode field should contain the barcode of the computer or other piece of equipment that has the problem you are describing. If the device does not have a barcode but does have a serial number, use the Serial field instead. If there is no barcode do not put anything in the Barcode field.
The Serial field should contain the serial number of the equipment experiencing the problem. If this is not relevant, leave it blank.
The Contact field should contain the name of the person who should be contacted who will have first hand knowledge of the problem and be able to explain it in detail. This field is required.
The Phone field should contain the phone number of either the contact person (preferred) or the author. This may be left blank.
The Email field should contain the email address of either the contact person (preferred) or the author. This must be a valid email address. This field is required.
The Location field should indicate the room in which the equipment which is experiencing trouble resides. Or, it should describe the area where the problem is felt, or most felt. Additionally, it could specify which piece of equipment in the room/area is experiencing the problem, or experiencing it the most, if there is more than one piece of equipment in the designated area which could be confused with the affected equipment.
The Priority field is for classifying the urgency of the problem being described. In almost all cases the priority will be "Normal" or less. If you are unsure of what the appropriate priority is use "Normal."
The Section field describes who the problem should be reported to. If you know for sure which section your problem is for, place it in that section. If you have any doubts at all, leave the section field untouched and that group will review it and make further adjustments if necessary.
The assigned technician field is for directly assigning tickets to a certain technician. This is useful for tickets related to something someone was already working on. If blank, a technician may be assigned by the administrator.
The Problem field should contain a verbose description of the problem you observed, including any details that may be important or seem related. This field is required.
The Troubleshooting Tried field should contain a description of all steps you took while attempting to resolve the Problem described above, as well as any results of that troubleshooting and a summary of where the problem now stands. This may be left blank.
The Date Free field should contain the date on which the contact person (preferred) or the author will be available should it be necessary to contact someone regarding the ticket. If left blank, the current date will be used.
The Free Time field should contain the time of day at which the contact person (preferred) or the author will be available should it be necessary to contact someone regarding the ticket. If left blank, the current time will be used.
The Notes field should contain any additional information for the technician. This may be left blank.
Tips in the 200 range are for the inventory side.
Tips in the 300 range are for administrative configuration.
The Section Name field should contain the name of the ticket queue as it will appear to users. It should be descriptive of what type of tickets will be assigned to it.
The Section Email field should contain the email address at which emails for that section should be received. It may be desirable to make it a distribution list.
Sites represent physical locations within a single company. For instance, the company "My Company" might have the sites "Main Offce" and "Satellite Office". This is useful for physically locating the equipment or user easily.
The Site Name field should contain a descriptive label for the physical location.
Site Level describes a category of physical locations (sites).
The Site Level Name field should contain a descriptive label for the type of physical location.
Compaines are used to segregate ownership of sites, users and equipment.
The Company Name field should contain the name of the new company you are adding.
